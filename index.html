HACT Programmatic Monitoring Visits Workflow

Role Glossary

PML — Programme Monitoring Lead: Leads and coordinates each field mission; may oversee multiple Programme Officers/monitors on a field visit mission.

PM — Programme Monitor: Member of the visit monitoring team; conducts fieldwork in coordination with PML.

PMO — Planning & Monitoring Officer (Field Office).

MO — Monitoring Officer (Country Office).

PMR — Planning/Monitoring & Research (oversight): Monthly generation and semi‑annual synthesis/analytics.

Step 1: Generation & Informing

Generate and distribute the HACT PMV Requirements (segregated by section and Field Office); to PMOs & CO focal points.

MO

Timeframe: Monthly (end of month)

Output: Required PMV List

Notes: HACT Requirements shared.

↓
Step 2: Planning & Submitting

Prioritise PMVs; prepare plans; submit for clearances; assign TPM for inaccessible areas.

PML (with PMs/PMOs)

Timeframe: 5 working days

Output: Scheduled PMV Plans

Notes: Plans include sites, tentative dates, participant monitors, checklists.

↓
Step 3: Clearances & Rolling‑out Mission

Obtain programme/admin/security clearances; confirm logistics; finalise itinerary; record go/no‑go.

PML

Timeframe: ≤5 working days

Output: Permitted Movement Plan & approvals

Notes: Access approvals acquired.

↓
Step 4: Data Collection

Conduct on‑site verification at community/facility level; capture notes, checklists, findings evidence; initiate on‑spot actions.

PML PMs

Timeframe: ~2 working days (per mission)

Output: Fieldwork Notes & Checklists

Notes: Evidence pack: notes, photos (time‑stamped), attendance, consent (as applicable).

↓
Step 5: Reporting to FMM

Compile and enter collected data and findings; link evidence; reflect on‑spot & scheduled actions.

PML PMs

Timeframe: 2 working days

Output: Draft PMV data and records reflected in FMM

Notes: Draft report submitted with attachments.

↓
Step 6: Consolidating & Submitting

Review, consolidate, and submit for QA review.

PML

Timeframe: 1 working day

Output: PMV Actionable Report (Ready for QA)

Notes: Status set to “Ready for QA.”

↓
Step 7: QA Review

QA checklist (incl. review of methods, valid verification, and actionability).

PMO MO

Timeframe: 2 working days

Output: Reviewed PMV Report

Notes: Outcome: “QA Passed” or “Revisions Required.”

↓
Step 8: Revision & Resubmission

Reflect QA reviews; revise report and actions; resubmit for QA confirmation.

PML PMs

Timeframe: 2 working days

Output: Revised & Quality‑Assured PMV Report

Notes: Quality reports with owned actions & recorded due dates.

↓
Step 9: Completion

Mark QA‑passed report as Complete; notify recipients.

PMO MO

Timeframe: ≤2 working days

Output: Completed, Quality‑Assured Report

Notes: HACT Requirements Met

↓
Step 10: Action Closure + Data use, Analysis & Dissemination

Track action implementation; analyse trends; produce monitoring Snapshots & Key Messages; inform PMT/PCM/CMT.

PMR oversight PMO MO

Timeframe: ~Semi‑annual

Output: Snapshots & Key Messages; Action‑closure tracker

Notes: Monitoring informed and data used adaptation
